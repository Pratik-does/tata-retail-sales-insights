Tata / Forage Data Visualisation: Empowering Business with Effective Insight

This project was built to convert raw retail transaction data into clear, decision-ready business insights for leadership stakeholders. The primary objective was to support executive decision-making by identifying revenue patterns, high-value customers, demand concentration, and market expansion opportunities through structured data cleaning and visual analysis.

The dataset was first cleaned to ensure accuracy and reliability for analysis. Invalid transactions were removed by filtering out records with negative quantities and non-positive unit prices. This step helped eliminate return transactions and pricing errors that could distort business interpretation. After cleaning, the data was prepared for analysis by creating revenue-based measures and organizing the dataset for time-series, customer-level, and geographic exploration.

The analysis was designed around business questions relevant to senior management. A revenue trend view was created to understand monthly performance in 2011 and identify seasonal demand patterns. A country-level comparison was developed to highlight the top-performing markets outside the United Kingdom and assess international revenue distribution. Customer-level analysis was used to identify the highest-value customers and understand revenue concentration. A geographic demand view was also created to show where demand was strongest and where expansion opportunities may exist.

The final dashboard was structured as an executive summary, making it easy for business leaders to scan key insights quickly. Each visual was paired with a concise insight sentence so the dashboard did not just present data, but also communicated meaning and action. This approach helped transform the analysis from a technical exercise into a business-focused narrative.

Overall, the project demonstrates a practical end-to-end data visualization workflow: cleaning raw data, selecting the right visual for each question, and presenting findings in a way that supports strategic decision-making. It reflects skills in data preparation, business understanding, dashboard design, and insight communication, which are essential for a junior data analyst or business intelligence role.

Questions Answered

The project addressed the following business questions:

What is the monthly revenue trend for 2011?
Which are the top 10 countries by revenue and quantity, excluding the United Kingdom?
Who are the top 10 customers by revenue?
Which countries show the highest global demand, excluding the United Kingdom?
